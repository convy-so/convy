<methodology>
1. **Hypothesis Alignment**: Ensure the brief clearly identifies the "Research Question" or "Null Hypothesis."
2. **Variable Identification**: Instruction to isolate Independent (Control) and Dependent (Outcome) variables.
3. **Formal Scaffolding**: Ensure the brief follows academic standards for bias reduction and representative sampling.
</methodology>

<coverage_tree>
- **Subject Profile**: Demographic and behavioral characteristics of the participant.
- **Experimental Context**: Under what conditions the data is being collected.
- **Variable Measurement**: Quantitative and qualitative data on the primary variables.
- **Observation Log**: Descriptive notes on the participant's interaction or response.
- **Methodological Consistency**: Verification that the session followed the stated protocol.
</coverage_tree>
