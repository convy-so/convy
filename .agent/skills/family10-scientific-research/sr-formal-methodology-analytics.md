# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Formal Methodology data is the UX audit of the knowledge economy. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify exactly where the "Business of Science" is physically stopping the "Progress of Science." It must differentiate between necessary rigor (e.g., double-blind peer review) and toxic bureaucracy (e.g., a 40-page grant application that only has a 2% funding rate). If a university, publisher, or software provider is being evaluated, the Analytics agent must explicitly warn them if their systems are causing top-tier talent to abandon the field.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Academic Matrix:**
- High Prestige, Low Friction: The Ivory Tower. Rare. Fully tenured, well-funded researchers who don't care about the bureaucracy.
- High Prestige, High Friction: The Grinder. PIs at top institutions who are completely miserable, spending 80 hours a week just maintaining the funding machine.
- Low Prestige, Low Friction: The Private Sector Pivot. Researchers who left academia for industry. They lost the "Prestige," but they actually get to do science again.
- Low Prestige, High Friction: The Perma-Post-Doc. The most vulnerable cohort. Massively underpaid, highly stressed, executing the bulk of the actual labor for someone else's citations. Guaranteed eventual churn.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Metric Corruption' Multiplier:** Feedback where a researcher explicitly states they altered their research strategy (e.g., salami-slicing a paper, choosing a "safe" topic over an innovative one, forcing positive results) not because it was good science, but because it was required to get funded/published, is weighted at 5x. This is the ultimate systemic failure.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The "Passion Tax."** The Analytics agent must report on the "Passion Tax"—the amount of structural abuse and low pay a researcher is willing to tolerate simply because they love their field of study. The report must explicitly warn clients that the Passion Tax is no longer sustainable for younger demographics.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Institutional layer evaluated (Publishing, Grant Writing, Lab Ops), demographics.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Academic Matrix (e.g., "A Systemic Cultivation of Burnout").
**Section 3: The 'Metric Corruption' Report**
Identification of exactly how the evaluation systems (e.g., Impact Factor, Grant KPIs) are damaging the actual science output.
**Section 4: The Bureaucratic Friction Audit**
A granular heat-map of wasted time (e.g., 20 hours a week formatting citations vs 5 hours analyzing data).
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the software changes, policy updates, or submission portal rebuilds requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Industry Defection Rate." If transcripts show that 80% of PhD candidates in the cohort are actively planning to leave academia the moment they graduate due to the toxic funding structure, the Analytics agent must flag this as an existential threat to the university client's long-term viability.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Metric Corruption' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the methodology data reveals a severe 'Metric Corruption' epidemic within the evaluated cohort. Across [Percentage]% of researchers, the institutional pressure to optimize for 'Impact Factor' and citation volume has actively superseded the pursuit of rigorous, innovative science. Researchers admitted to designing smaller, derivative, 'safe' experiments specifically because they are easier to fund and faster to publish. The current institutional incentives are not filtering for quality; they are filtering for compliance. We strongly advise the funding/publishing bodies involved to radically alter their evaluation KPIs to reward data transparency and null-hypothesis publication over sheer volume."
