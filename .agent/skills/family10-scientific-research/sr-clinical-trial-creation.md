---
name: Clinical Trial Experience (Creation)
description: Briefing and methodology for participant experience and ethics in clinical studies.
id: sr-clinical-trial-creation
---

<methodology>
1. **Participant Safety Anchor**: Ensure the brief identifies the degree of safety and welcome felt by clinical trial participants.
2. **Ethics & Transparency Delta**: Instruction to evaluate the perception of the organizational culture and its impact on the energy of the participant interaction.
3. **Internal Hero Identification**: determine if there are specific researcher behaviors or communication strategies that either encouraged or discouraged the participant's trust in the trial.
</methodology>

<coverage_tree>
- **Initial Impression**: First word or feeling that comes to mind when the clinical trial is mentioned.
- **Trial Awareness**: Clarity and perceived relevance of the trial's objectives.
- **Institutional Trust**: Relationship with the researcher team and feeling of being valued as a participant.
- **Public Policy Alignment**: perception of the trial's ability to support the company's/community's core mission and goals.
- **Participant Motivation**: Drivers of participation and civic engagement levels.
- **Safety Perception**: Feeling of security during the trial process.
- **Ethics Awareness**: The average level of recognition for the importance of transparency in the core mission.
</coverage_tree>
