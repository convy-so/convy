# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Audience Fragmentation data dictates the physical format of a media company's output. It answers the question: *Is this a movie, a 6-part series, a podcast, or a TikTok account?* If the Analytics agent discovers that the target demographic has entirely lost the cognitive patience for 45-minute audio segments, the client's new flagship standard-length podcast will fail, regardless of how good the host is. The agent must synthesize the transcripts to clearly delineate between "Active Focus" formats and "Passive Companion" formats.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Attention Matrix:**
- High Focus, High Friction (Prestige TV/Movies): Requires a massive marketing pull to convince the user to commit the undivided time.
- Low Focus, High Friction (Long-form Podcasts/Streams): Used as "Companion Media" while performing chores or commuting.
- High Focus, Low Friction (Viral Short-form): The algorithmic trap. Maximum adrenaline, zero loyalty.
- Low Focus, Low Friction (Background Music/Ambient): Pure utility. Completely commoditized.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Second-Screen' Multiplier:** Feedback explicitly detailing what the user is doing on their *phone* while the primary media is playing (e.g., "I always play that podcast while doing the dishes") is weighted at 4x. This proves the media is serving a utility ("Chore Companion") rather than an artistic need, fundamentally altering how it should be monetized.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The 'Stated vs Revealed' Preference Gap:** The Analytics agent must explicitly measure the gap between what users *claim* they want (e.g., "I want more high-intellect documentaries") and what they *revealed* they actually consume (e.g., "I watched 4 hours of TikTok drama summaries last night"). The Analytics report must base all strategic recommendations entirely on the *revealed* behavior, actively counseling the client to ignore the stated preference.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Media formats evaluated, demographic bounds.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Cohort's Attention Span (e.g., "Terminal Algorithm Fatigue").
**Section 3: The 'Active vs Passive' Ratio**
A calculation of how much of the client's media is being actively watched versus serving as background noise.
**Section 4: The Format Exhaustion Audit**
Identification of specific run-times or formats (e.g., "The 3-hour podcast") that the cohort is actively abandoning.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the format pivots, content clipping strategies, or production budget reallocations requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for "Algorithmic Resentment." If respondents universally state that they spend 2 hours a day on a platform but feel actively "gross" or "exhausted" afterward, the Analytics agent must flag this platform as highly vulnerable to a "Digital Detox" mass exodus, advising the client not to index their entire marketing strategy on it.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Prestige Fatigue' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the audience data reveals a terminal case of 'Prestige Television Fatigue' within the target demographic. While respondents theoretically claimed an interest in complex, serialized narratives, behavioral reality mapping showed that [Percentage]% of the cohort functionally requires 'Second-Screen Compatible' media. They explicitly rejected the client's new series because 'it required too much focus' and 'couldn't be followed while scrolling Instagram.' We strongly advise the network to stop investing in serialized, dense mysteries for this specific cohort, and pivot production into episodic, low-cognitive-load programming that can survive divided attention spans."
