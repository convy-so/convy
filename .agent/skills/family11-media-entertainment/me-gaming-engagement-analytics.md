# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Gaming data is notoriously deceptive. A game can boast 5 million "Daily Active Users," but if 4 million of them are logging in purely out of "Fear Of Missing Out" (FOMO) rather than actual enjoyment, the IP is a ticking time bomb. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify the "Burnout Horizon." It must differentiate between healthy monetization (players buying cosmetics because they love the game) and predatory monetization (players buying advantages because the game is intentionally too hard without them). 

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Engagement Matrix:**
- High Fun, Healthy Economy: The Golden Goose. High retention, high organic spend.
- High Fun, Toxic Economy: The Hostage Situation. Players love the mechanics but hate the developer. The Reddit community will be violently toxic, but DAU remains high until a competitor arrives.
- Low Fun, Healthy Economy: The Ghost Town. Great consumer-friendly practices, but the game is boring.
- Low Fun, Toxic Economy: The Cash Grab. Exists entirely to drain 'Whales' before the server shuts down.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Predatory' Multiplier:** Feedback explicitly describing a monetization mechanic as "Pay-to-Win" or "Gambling/Predatory" is weighted at 5x. When the gaming community universally adopts this narrative, it becomes a permanent brand stain that takes years to wash off.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The Core Loop Base:** The Analytics agent must always contextualize complaints against the "Fun Factor" of the Core Loop. If the players complain endlessly about the menus and the store, but universally admit the core shooting/driving is the best in the industry, the game can easily be saved with UI patches. If the core loop is fundamentally boring, no amount of store patching matters.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Game model (Live Service vs Single-Player), target player tier.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Engagement Matrix (e.g., "A Hostage Situation with pending Churn").
**Section 3: The 'Fun vs Grind' Tipping Point**
Calculation of exactly how many hours it takes before players view the game as a "chore."
**Section 4: The Monetization Fairness Audit**
Analysis of whether the current store prices are causing brand damage or are accepted by the community.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the battle-pass changes, mechanic reworks, or community management strategies requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Sunk Cost Loyalty." If the majority of veteran players actively advise new players *not* to start playing the game, but continue to play it themselves because they "already put 1,000 hours into it," the Analytics agent must flag that the game has zero organic growth potential and is entirely dependent on its decaying veteran base.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Burnout' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the engagement data reveals a systemic 'Burnout Horizon' threatening the Live Service model. While quantitative DAU metrics remain stable, qualitative analysis shows that [Percentage]% of the core demographic is playing exclusively under 'FOMO Duress' rather than organic enjoyment. Respondents universally characterized the current Battle Pass progression curve as 'a second job' designed to artificially inflate playtime. This model predicts a massive, permanent churn event at the conclusion of the current season. We strongly advise immediately reducing the XP requirements for progression by at least 30% to re-introduce 'recreational margin' into the player's session."
