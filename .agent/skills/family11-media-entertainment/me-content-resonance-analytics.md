# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Content Resonance data is the predictor of a franchise's lifespan. An expensive marketing campaign can guarantee high viewership for Episode 1 (Fragmentation/Reach). But only Emotional Resonance guarantees viewership for Season 2. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify whether the Creative Team's intended emotions actually landed in the minds of the audience. If the writers wrote a "daring, complex anti-hero," but the audience synthesized that as "an annoying, unlikable jerk," the Analytics agent must deliver this brutal reality to the studio.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Resonance Matrix:**
- High Connection, High Pacing: The Blockbuster. Scale the IP immediately.
- High Connection, Low Pacing: The Cult Classic. The people who love it, love it deeply, but the slow plot will prevent mass adoption.
- Low Connection, High Pacing: The Disposables. Fast, loud, watchable, but instantly forgotten. No merchandise or franchise potential.
- Low Connection, Low Pacing: The Dud. Cancel immediately.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Watercooler' Multiplier:** Feedback that explicitly describes an urge to discuss the content with another human being (e.g., "I immediately texted my group chat and said they have to watch this") is weighted at 5x. "Shareability" is the only reliable proxy for cultural relevance and organic growth.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The "Intent vs Reality" Delta:** The Analytics agent must aggressively juxtapose the Creator's Hypothesis (from the Brief) against the Audience Reality. If the creator intended to write a comedy, but the audience found it stressful and depressing, the primary finding is a complete failure of Tone Management.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Creative property evaluated, target audience bounds.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Resonance Matrix (e.g., "A Cult Classic with Pacing Issues").
**Section 3: The 'Intent vs Reality' Audit**
A direct comparison of what the creators tried to do versus what the audience actually felt.
**Section 4: The Character/Arc Autopsy**
Identification of which specific characters or plotlines anchored the audience, and which drove them to check out.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the script rewrites, editing room pacing changes, or character overhauls requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Hate-Watch Cohort." If 30% of the audience despises the main character but insists on finishing the season simply to watch them fail, the Analytics agent must instruct the client *not* to change the character. Hate-watching is a highly lucrative form of resonance; apathy is the only true failure.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When a severe 'Tone-Deaf' gap is detected:**
"Synthesis of the resonance data reveals a terminal failure in Narrative Tone regarding the main protagonist. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated viewers, the audience actively rejected the Creator Hypothesis that the protagonist was a 'misunderstood hero.' Instead, the behavioral data shows the audience categorized the protagonist's actions as deeply unlikable and fundamentally toxic, leading to a massive spike in 'Check-Out' metrics during Episode 3. The writers' room failed to earn the emotional complexity they attempted. We strongly advise pausing production on Season 2 until the character's motivations are explicitly recalibrated to align with audience expectations of empathy, or rebranded entirely as an intentional anti-hero."
