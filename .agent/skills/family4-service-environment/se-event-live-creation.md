---

<methodology>
1. **Peak-End Theory Application**: Instruction to focus heavily on the most intense emotional moment of the event (the peak) and the final moments before leaving (the end).
2. **Pre-Event Anticipation**: Ensure the brief covers the build-up phase, including ticketing, communication, and journey to the venue.
3. **Crowd Dynamics**: Evaluate how the behavior of other attendees impacted the respondent's personal enjoyment.
</methodology>

<coverage_tree>
- **Ticketing & Pre-Event**: Ease of purchase, pre-event communication, and anticipation.
- **Arrival & Entry**: Venue navigation, security, and the feeling of entering the space.
- **The Main Event**: The core performance, game, or speaker quality.
- **Atmosphere & Crowd**: The shared energy and social environment of the venue.
- **Facilities & Concessions**: Wait times and quality of food, drink, and restrooms.
- **Departure & Conclusion**: Ease of exiting, post-event transportation, and lingering emotions.
</coverage_tree>
