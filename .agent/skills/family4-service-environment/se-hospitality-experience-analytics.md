## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Distinguish "Hard Product" (Building/Bed) from "Soft Product" (Service/Warmth).
**Type A (Sanctuary Failure):** Environment failed to provide rest (Noise, AC, Bed). Impact: Destroys return intent.
**Type B (Service Failure):** Staff failed to provide care (Dismissive, slow response). Impact: Soft product failure.
**Type C (Atmospheric):** "Vibe," Pool beauty, Modern lobby. Impact: Luxury bonus; does not compensate for A or B.
**Service Compensation:** When "nice staff" masks a "broken room." Finding: High-risk operational drift.

## Critical Misreadings
- **SOP Compliance:** Staff was "professional/polite" but not proactive = No genuine hospitality.
- **Silent Friction:** Small issues guests don't complain about (Light switches, UX) = Brand value erosion.
- **Destination Association:** Happy because of the City (Paris!), not the Hotel. Isolate "On-Property."

## Evidence Hierarchy
| Tier | Title | Evidence Type |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Sanctuary/Service Instance (1.00) | Specific: "Elevator motor vibrated through wall at 3AM." |
| Tier 2 | Comparative Service (0.90) | Contrast: "Warm breakfast crew vs. dismissive front desk." |
| Tier 3 | Expectation Gap (0.80) | Marketing vs. Reality: "Image showed balcony, was just a rail." |
| Tier 4 | Directional Pattern (0.60) | General: "Property felt tired" or "Staff seemed overwhelmed." |
| Tier 5 | Atmospheric (0.45) | "Nice stay," "Good vibe" (Weighted at 0.65; contextual). |

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| The Tired Property | High Service / Low Sanctuary | Soft product masking facility obsolescence. |
| The Cold Flagship | High Sanctuary / Low (Scripted) Service | Facility is perfect but hospitality is robotic. |
| Frictionless Factory | High Consistency / Low Warmth | Operational efficiency without emotional connection. |
| Recovery Paradox | Failure (A/B) resolved with Genuine Care | Loyalty is higher but failure reported at full value. |

## Scoring Weighting
- **Recovery Bonus (+0.15):** Applied to loyalty if failure was resolved with "Genuine Care."
- **Recency Penalty (-0.10):** Apply to non-arrival nodes if session >48h after stay.

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Executive Summary** (The Sanctuary/Service Balance).
**Part 2: Structural Findings** (The Stay Arc: Arrival to Departure).
**Part 3: Recommendations** (CapEx/Physical vs. OpEx/Training).
**Part 4: Limitations** (Recency bias, trip context).

## Escalation Protocols
- **Total Sanctuary Failure:** Sleep impossible due to noise/climate/safety.
- **Critical Service Breach:** Dismissive, rude, or discriminatory behavior.

## Reporting Flag Templates
**RECENCY BIAS:**
> "This session was conducted [X] days after stay completion. Findings on 'Arrival & Transition' may be subject to recency fading and should be compared against real-time check-in logs."

**SERVICE COMPENSATION:**
> "The guest reported high overall satisfaction despite clear physical failures in 'Room Sanctuary.' This is a 'Service Compensation' finding. The soft product is currently protecting the brand from facility obsolescence."
