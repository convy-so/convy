## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Distinguish operational realities (weather/traffic) from organizational failures (info/cleanliness).
**Principle:** Agency vs. Information. Disruption of "Arc" balance against "Update" quality.
**Anticipation (SE-01):** Pre-trip control. Digital interface ease.
**Sanctuary (SE-03):** Physical decompression space. Vehicle cleanliness/comfort.
**The Gap (SE-04):** Operational transparency. Silence during disruption is a fatal trust failure.
**Hostage of Geography:** Coercive Loyalty. Usage driven by hub monopoly/price floor, not preference.

## Critical Misreadings
- **Blaming the Weather:** A 4-hour delay with 15-min updates is a **success** in trust preservation. Audit the communication gap, not the storm.
- **Friendly over Accurate:** Professionalism is secondary to accuracy (info) and sanctuary (cleanliness).
- **Frequency vs. Loyalty:** High usage/Commuter status often masks deep cynicism. Check "Competitor Switch" for true sentiment.

## Evidence Hierarchy
| Tier | Title | Evidence Type |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Info Blackout (1.00) | Specific: "On tarmac for 90 mins and pilot didn't intercom once." |
| Tier 2 | Safety Signal (0.95) | Specific: "Driver looking at phone repeatedly while on highway." |
| Tier 3 | Departure Friction (0.85) | Mismatch: "App said Gate B12, signs said B14, agent didn't know." |
| Tier 4 | Aesthetic/Comfort (0.60) | Physical: "Legroom was better than expected" or "Cabin was clean." |
| Tier 5 | Generic Social (0.35) | Noise: "Smooth ride" / "Crew was nice" (Weighted 0.65; contextual). |

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| The Transparent Struggle | High Delays / High Transparency | Trust Retained; operational failure recovered by communication. |
| The Silent Slickness | Low Delays / Low Transparency | Trust Fragile; success is brittle without info safety net. |
| The Hostage Provider | Monopoly Route / Low Sanctuary | Coercive Loyalty; high churn risk if competitor enters. |
| Informational Blackout | Period of silence during disruption | Fatal trust failure; abandonment perception. |

## Scoring Weighting
- **Stakes Multiplier (x2.0 on SE-04):** Double communication gap severity for High-Stakes (Meeting/Funeral) trips.
- **Arrival Relief Discount (-0.15):** Subtract 0.15 from final sentiment if Tier 1/2 evidence was present but later forgiven due to arrival.

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Executive Summary** (The Anxiety vs. Agency Balance).
**Part 2: Structural Findings** (Communication Gaps & Sanctuary Failures).
**Part 3: Operational Risk** (Monopoly Resentment & Safety Signals).
**Part 4: Recommendations** (SMS/App logic vs. Fleet vs. Training).

## Escalation Protocols
- **Safety/Liability Incident:** Dangerous driving, mechanical failure, terminal/cabin threats.
- **Systemic Informational Blackout:** Multiple travelers left tanpa updates for >60 mins.

## Reporting Flag Templates
**INFORMATIONAL BLACKOUT:**
> "The data reveals a critical 'Informational Blackout' (SE-04). While the delay was weather-related, the failure to provide updates for [X] minutes resulted in an exponential spike in negative sentiment. Trust preservation failed due to communication."

**COERCIVE LOYALTY:**
> "The respondent exhibits 'Coercive Loyalty.' High usage is driven by [Monopoly/Price], not brand preference. Sentiment toward SE-03 (Sanctuary) is consistently low, suggesting immediate churn if a 'Voluntary Choice' competitor enters."
