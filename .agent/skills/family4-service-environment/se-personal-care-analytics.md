## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Identify the "Chair Freeze" (Vulnerability-induced silence).
**Principle:** Measure power dynamic between Client and Professional.
**Consultation (SE-02):** Foundation of trust score. Was it a collaboration or a lecture?
**Boundary (SE-03):** Reading cues. Includes "Conversational Tax" (forced small talk).
**The Reveal (SE-04):** Did the technical result match the consultation benchmark?
**High-Risk State:** High Technical / Low Trust. The client loved the result but hated the experience; they will churn for a "good enough" technician who makes them feel better.

## Critical Misreadings
- **The Polite Hostage:** Clients smile and tip while under the cape/sheet to avoid conflict. Real satisfaction is "Request for Specific Professional" (SE-06).
- **The Authority Trap:** A technically perfect result that wasn't what the client asked for is a 0/10 operational failure. Do not award points for Technical Perfection if Alignment was missed.
- **The Poison Pill:** 60 min relaxation ruined by a 5 min high-pressure retail pitch at checkout (SE-05).

## Evidence Hierarchy
| Tier | Title | Evidence Type |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | "Chair Freeze" (1.00) | Confession: "I realized it was too short but was too nervous to speak up." |
| Tier 2 | Boundary Failure (0.95) | Specific: "Stylist talked for 40 mins despite my eyes being closed." |
| Tier 3 | Consultation Contra. (0.85) | Alignment Delta: "Showed a photo of X, but they did Y." |
| Tier 4 | Sensory Outcome (0.60) | Physical: "Skin felt tight" or "The cut is even." |
| Tier 5 | Generic Social (0.35) | Noise: "They were friendly" (Weighted 0.65; contextual). |

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| The Invisible Artist | High Result / High Boundary (Silent) | Optimal for relaxation/introverted clients. |
| The Exhausting Expert | High Result / Low Boundary (Talkative) | Churn risk despite technical skill. |
| The Polished Failure | High Social / Low Technical Alignment | Misplaced hospitality; didn't listen to the brief. |
| The Mirror Moment | Positive immediate sentiment vs. later regret | Inflated by professional "fishing" for compliments. |

## Scoring Weighting
- **Transformation Multiplier (x1.5 on SE-02):** Increase consultation weight for "High Stakes" services (Color/Injectables).
- **"The Mirror" Discount (-0.10):** Subtract if the professional fished for compliments during reveal.

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Executive Summary** (The Trust vs. Result Balance).
**Part 2: Structural Findings** (Consultation Gaps & Boundary Friction).
**Part 3: Retention Risk** (Professional vs. Brand loyalty).
**Part 4: Recommendations** (Soft-skills vs. Technical training).

## Escalation Protocols
- **Physical Safety/Pain:** Chemical burns, extreme pain, hygiene issues.
- **Consent/Boundary Breach:** Feeling "trapped," "bullied," or inadequate physical covering.

## Reporting Flag Templates
**SOCIAL DESIRABILITY:**
> "The respondent exhibited high 'Social Desirability' generalities. However, the Tier 1 confession regarding 'Chair Freeze' (SE-04) suggests the technical outcome was not aligned with their desire."

**CONVERSATIONAL TAX:**
> "The data shows a high 'Conversational Tax' (SE-03). The client reported high technical satisfaction but noted exhaustion from forced interaction. This is a primary driver for 'Silent Churn'."
