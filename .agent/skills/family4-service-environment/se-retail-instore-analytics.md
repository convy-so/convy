## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Measure physical store justification against "Digital Substitution." 
**Principle:** Balance trip effort (Parking, Lines, Crowd) against sensory reward (Immediacy, Tactility, Service).
**The Mission (SE-01):** Targeted Buyer (low friction tolerance) vs. Browser (high discovery tolerance).
**Product Halo:** When item satisfaction masks a messy environment or rude staff. Action: Isolate Inventory from Environment.
**High-Risk State:** High Navigation Friction (SE-02) + High Digital Utility (availability online) = Brick-and-Mortary Attrition.

## Critical Misreadings
- **Captured Customer:** High ATV despite 20-min lines/dirty rooms. Result: Transactional success, Brand failure.
- **Friendly vs. Helpful:** Politeness is secondary to staff Utility (finding sizes, advice).
- **Checkout Churn:** Descriptive "Silent Abandonment" (dropping items) signifies fatale revenue leakage at POS.

## Evidence Hierarchy
| Tier | Title | Evidence Type |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Substitution Regret (1.00) | "I stood in line and thought: 'I should have ordered this online'." |
| Tier 2 | Spatial Friction (0.95) | Specific: "Front displays so crowded I couldn't get a stroller through." |
| Tier 3 | "Hover vs. Ignore" (0.85) | Inconsistency: "3 people greeted me, but all vanished for a size." |
| Tier 4 | Fitting Room Sensory (0.65) | Obstacle: "Harsh lighting made me not want to buy the dress." |
| Tier 5 | Atmospheric (0.40) | General: "Store was nice and bright" (Weighted 0.65; contextual). |

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| The Museum Store | High Aesthetics / Low Staff Utility | High Substitution Risk; visual win, commercial fail. |
| The Warehouse Grind | High Inventory / High Spatial Friction | High Effort; transactional win, loyalty fail. |
| Frictionless Flagship | Low Effort / High Discovery | Optimal spatial and social flow. |
| Revenue Leakage | Mentions of "Leaving" due to lines/mess | Fatal POS failure; abandoned missions. |

## Scoring Weighting
- **Mission Multiplier (x1.5 on SE-05):** Checkout friction weighted higher for high-urgency shoppers.
- **Product Halo Discount (-0.20):** Discount environmental sentiment if exclusive inventory was purchased.

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Executive Summary** (The Trip vs. Click Balance).
**Part 2: Structural Findings** (Spatial Logic & Staff Utility).
**Part 3: Revenue Leakage** (Abandoned Missions & Checkout Friction).
**Part 4: Recommendations** (VM vs. Training vs. Tech).

## Escalation Protocols
- **Loss Prevention Friction:** Feeling "trapped" or "profiled" leading to immediate exit.
- **Systemic Checkout Failure:** POS systems down or lines >15 mins during off-peak.

## Reporting Flag Templates
**DIGITAL SUBSTITUTION:**
> "The findings indicate a critical 'Digital Substitution' risk. While respondents successfully purchased items, the 'Effort Score' of the physical trip (SE-02/SE-05) was rated lower than the perceived convenience of the brand's e-commerce channel."

**PRODUCT HALO:**
> "Overall sentiment is artificially inflated by 'Product Halo' (satisfaction with a specific item). Deep nodes (SE-02/03) reveal significant spatial friction and staff unavailability that was merely tolerated."
