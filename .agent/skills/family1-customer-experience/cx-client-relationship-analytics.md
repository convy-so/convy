## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Audit professional trust and strategic value. Identify the "Managed Satisfaction" trap.
**Key Metric:** **Person-Institution Divergence.** Is the health in the AM (Individual) or the Brand (Institution)?
**Synthesis Rule:** A relationship that is positive but declining is more urgent than a negative but stable one.
**Quality Rule:** Strategic anchors (specific proactive instances) are the #1 data value.

## Critical Misreadings
- **The Loyalty Buffer:** Identifying when personal liking for an AM masks institutional failure.
- **Managed Satisfaction:** Adequate scoring without any functional/emotional investment.
- **Strategic Leverage:** Recognizing when answers are shaped by active contract negotiations.
- **Reactive Competence:** Conflating "good at tasks" with "strategic partnership."

## Evidence Hierarchy
| Tier | Title | Evidence Type |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Behavioral (1.00) | Specific proactive instance named (Anticipated a need). |
| Tier 2 | Comparative (0.90) | Differentiator identified vs. Competitors/Past providers. |
| Tier 3 | Resilience (0.85) | High-stakes substitution test (Confirms person-dependence). |
| Tier 4 | Compliance (0.60) | General professional adequacy (Standard task execution). |
| Tier 5 | Pulse (0.45) | "Good people" / "Like them" (Emotional only; no business proof).|

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| AM-High / Inst-Low | Personal trust > Delivery reliability | **CRITICAL.** Succession Risk. Build institutional depth. |
| AM-Low / Inst-High | Delivery is solid but AM is a bad fit | Personnel Change: Immediate replacement opportunity. |
| Reactive Health | High competence score / Low proactivity | Competitive Risk: Relationship is strategically substitutable.|
| Managed Sat. | Pulse-positive / 0 Value anchors | Retention Risk: Relationship held by switching cost only. |

## Synthesis Protocols
- **Step 1 (Divergence Audit):** Compare Personnel (AM) vs. Institutional delivery. Flag gaps > 0.20.
- **Step 2 (Proactivity Classification):** Reactive (Standard) vs. Proactive (Healthy) vs. Anticipatory (Premium).
- **Step 3 (Vulnerability Mapping):** Cross-reference Person-Dependence with Competitive Awareness.
- **Step 4 (Readiness):** Identify unaddressed pain points for expansion potential.

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Executive Summary** (Health characterization, Direction, and Divergence factor).
**Part 2: Relationship Health** (Emotional quality vs. Professional adequacy).
**Part 3: Account Manager Assessment** (Trust/Chemistry vs. Proactive Understanding).
**Part 4: Institutional Assessment** (Reliability vs. Strategic Value).
**Part 5: Resilience Assessment** (Person-Dependence Risk & Expansion Opportunity).

## Flagging Templates
**PERSON-DEPENDENCE RISK:**
> "The session confirms a person-dependent relationship: health is primarily attributable to [Name]'s performance. Switching risk is [High/Med] if [Name] moves on. Recommended: Institutional depth-building."

**MANAGED SATISFACTION:**
> "Relationship is adequate but lacks emotional investment or specific value anchors. Held by switching cost, not preference. Retention risk in the event of pricing/AM change."

**LEVERAGE DYNAMIC:**
> "Session conducted during active negotiation. Negative findings on commercial dimensions may be negotiation-motivated. Corroborate outside negotiation context."

**DIVERGENCE LIMITATION:**
> "Person-institution separation could not be achieved. Respondent blended assessments throughout. Findings reported as blended relationship health."
