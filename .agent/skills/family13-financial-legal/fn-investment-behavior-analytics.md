# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Investment Behavior data is the psychological audit of a financial platform. An investing platform can only survive long-term if its users survive long-term. If the platform's UI encourages reckless gambling, the users will eventually go broke and churn permanently. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to measure the "Risk Literacy Gap"—the delta between how much risk a demographic *thinks* they are taking, versus how much risk they are *actually* taking. The Analytics agent must aggressively flag "Gamification Toxicity."

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Investor Behavioral Matrix:**
- High Literacy, High Risk: The Calculated Speculator. They know the risks, they accept the losses. Stable highly-active user.
- High Literacy, Low Risk: The Steady Accumulator. The backbone of AUM (Assets Under Management). Boring but highly profitable for the platform.
- Low Literacy, High Risk: The Casino Gambler. Extremely high engagement in the short term, but guaranteed to eventually lose their capital and churn. High regulatory risk.
- Low Literacy, Low Risk: The Intimidated Novice. High potential, but currently paralyzed by fear of making a mistake. Requires UX education.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Algorithm Blame' Multiplier:** Feedback where the user explicitly blames the platform's UI or notifications for causing a bad trade (e.g., "The app sent me a push alert that the stock was up 20%, so I panic-bought at the top") is weighted at 4x. This indicates that the app's engagement features are crossing the line into active manipulation in the mind of the user.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The "Euphoria vs Reality" Base:** The Analytics agent must determine if the cohort's engagement is tied entirely to a Bull Market. If respondents explicitly state they only check the app because "my stocks are going up," the Analytics report must explicitly warn the client that platform engagement will collapse by 80% the moment a bear market begins.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Asset class evaluated (Crypto, Stocks, Options), demographic bounds.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Behavioral Matrix (e.g., "A Dangerously High Concentration of Casino Gamblers").
**Section 3: The 'FOMO' Audit**
Specific measurement of how much trading activity is driven by social media/peer pressure rather than financial planning.
**Section 4: UX/Gamification Toxicity Log**
Granular list of mechanical features (animations, alerts, color schemes) that users admit caused irrational behavior.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the UI redesigns, educational gating, or marketing pivots requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Panic Threshold." If transcripts show that a hypothetical 15% market drop would cause 60% of the cohort to liquidate their entire portfolios to cash, the Analytics agent must report extreme fragility in the platform's Asset Under Management retention model.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Gamification Toxicity' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the behavioral data reveals a critical 'Gamification Toxicity' vulnerability within the novice cohort. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated users, the platform's UI design actively triggered 'Casino/Gambling' psychology rather than rational investment planning. Respondents explicitly noted that features like rapid push-notifications and 'hot streak' visuals caused them to execute trades they otherwise would have avoided. While this drives short-term transaction revenue, it guarantees a severe churn cliff when these users exhaust their capital. We strongly advise the Product team to implement 'Friction Bumps' (e.g., mandatory confirmation screens outlining the exact mathematical risk) on complex asset purchases to protect long-term retention and mitigate looming regulatory scrutiny."
