# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Legal Access data is the audit of "Cognitive Friction." LegalTech companies often erroneously believe their main value proposition is "saving the user money." The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to expose the reality: the main value proposition is actually "reducing anxiety." If a legal product is cheap but highly confusing, the user will abandon it because the anxiety of "doing it wrong" outweighs the savings. The Analytics agent must ruthlessly flag "Jargon Paralysis" and identify exactly where users lose trust in the automated system.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Access Matrix:**
- High Clarity, High Trust: The Peace-of-Mind Utility. The holy grail of LegalTech. Users feel safe and smart.
- Low Clarity, High Trust: The Blind Faith Model. Users don't understand the product, but they trust the brand. Highly vulnerable to a PR crisis or a competitor with better UX.
- High Clarity, Low Trust: The "Too Good to be True" Trap. The UX is flawless and cheap, but the user fundamentally does not believe a $50 app can actually protect them legally.
- Low Clarity, Low Trust: The Fraud Perception. Users assume the service is a scam designed to steal their data or overcharge them for useless forms.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Jargon Paralysis' Multiplier:** Feedback explicitly identifying a specific term, checkbox, or screen that caused the user to pause their session to use Google (e.g., "I didn't know if I needed a Registered Agent, so I just closed the tab") is weighted at 5x. This pinpoints the exact UX leak in the conversion funnel.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The "Human Premium" Base:** The Analytics agent must determine the threshold at which users demand human intervention. As legal stakes rise (e.g., incorporating a business vs getting a divorce), trust in automation collapses. The Analytics report must clearly define this threshold for the client's product roadmap.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Legal product evaluated (e.g., LLC formation, Estate Planning), demographic bounds.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Access Matrix (e.g., "Jargon Paralysis disrupting a High Trust product").
**Section 3: The 'Jargon Paralysis' Audit**
A granular list of the top 3 most confusing terms/screens that triggered cart abandonment or severe anxiety.
**Section 4: The 'Human vs Machine' Trust Threshold**
Analysis of exactly when and why the demographic demanded human legal counsel over automated tools.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the UX rewrites (plain English), pricing tier adjustments, or support integrations requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Post-Purchase Reassurance." If transcripts universally show that users complete the legal transaction but still feel highly anxious afterward ("I hope I did it right"), the Analytics agent must flag a failure in the product's 'Confirmation UX', advising the client to invest in post-purchase "Peace of Mind" messaging.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Jargon Paralysis' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the access data reveals a catastrophic 'Jargon Paralysis' failure at the midpoint of the conversion funnel. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated users, the sudden introduction of complex legal terminology regarding [Specific Topic] triggered massive cognitive overload and immediate cart abandonment. The product is currently functioning strictly as a tool for legally literate users, entirely failing its mandate to democratize access. We strongly advise an immediate UX rewrite of the intake flow, stripping all traditional legal terminology in favor of 'Plain English' conversational questions, supported by ubiquitous 'What does this mean?' tooltip definitions."
