# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Insurance Claims data is the ultimate measure of "Brand Promise vs Brand Reality." An insurance company's only product is a promise to help on the worst day of a customer's life. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify "The Adversary Pivot"—the moment the customer realized the company was actively trying to pay them the absolute minimum. If the Analytics agent detects widespread 'Bad Faith' perception, it means the marketing budget (which sells peace of mind) is entirely wasted, because the operational reality is adversarial.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Claims Matrix:**
- Fast, High Empathy: The Brand Evangelist. They had a crisis, the company fixed it instantly. They will never switch providers.
- Slow, High Empathy: The Forgiving Loyalist. The process was a mess, but "the adjuster was so nice and really fought for me." The human element saved the account.
- Fast, Low Empathy: The Cold Utility. The algorithm paid them instantly, but nobody asked if they were okay. Appreciated, but no emotional loyalty.
- Slow, Low Empathy: The Betrayal. Absolute worst-case scenario. Certain churn, high likelihood of negative online reviews and active brand damage.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Denial by Exhaustion' Multiplier:** Feedback indicating a customer actively gave up on a valid portion of their claim simply because the paperwork was too difficult is weighted at 5x. While this looks like a "savings" on the company's ledger, the Analytics agent must flag it as a catastrophic failure of the customer experience that guarantees churn.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The "Loyalty Illusion."** The Analytics agent must explicitly warn the client that a 10-year customer who never filed a claim is NOT a loyal customer; they just haven't tested the product yet. The only real measure of retention is what the customer does *after* the claim is closed.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Line of business (Auto, Home, Health, etc.), complexity of claims evaluated.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Claims Matrix (e.g., "A Terminal Case of Slow, Low Empathy Betrayal").
**Section 3: The 'Adversary Pivot' Audit**
Identification of the exact step (e.g., First Notice, Adjuster Visit, Final Check) where the relationship turned toxic.
**Section 4: The 'Empathy vs Efficiency' Report**
Analysis of whether the new digital/AI tools actually helped the customer or just made them feel isolated.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the paperwork reductions, adjuster script rewrites, or API integrations requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Communication Black Hole." If 80% of negative transcripts cite the exact same issue—"I submitted the photos and then nobody talked to me for three weeks"—the Analytics agent must flag this as the singular priority for the Operations team. Silence from an insurance company is always interpreted by the customer as a pending denial.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Bad Faith' perception is detected:**
"Synthesis of the claims data reveals a catastrophic 'Bad Faith' perception severely damaging post-claim retention. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated claimants, the company's stringent documentation requirements were not viewed as standard fraud-prevention, but as an intentional 'Denial by Exhaustion' strategy. Customers explicitly stated they felt 'treated like a criminal' by the adjusting team. While the claims may have been resolved within the legal SLA timeframe, the adversarial posture of the communication guarantees that these customers will switch carriers at their next renewal date. We strongly advise a complete overhaul of the adjuster communication scripts to prioritize advocacy over interrogation."
