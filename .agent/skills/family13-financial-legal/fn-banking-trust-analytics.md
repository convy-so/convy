# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Banking Trust data is the structural integrity scan of a financial institution. A bank can survive a bad mobile app, but it cannot survive a loss of trust. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify the difference between "True Growth" and "Trapped Customers." If a bank relies entirely on the friction of switching accounts to keep its customers, it is functionally a dying institution waiting for the technology to catch up. The Analytics agent must flag specific banking policies (e.g., overdrafts, minimum balances) that are generating toxic resentment.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Trust Matrix:**
- High Trust, Low Friction: The Advocate. They stay because they love it. Highly valuable for referral programs.
- Low Trust, High Friction: The Hostage. They hate the bank but are trapped by auto-pay and direct deposits. They will leave the instant a frictionless alternative appears.
- High Trust, High Friction: The Legacy User. Usually older demographics who trust the physical branch but hate the digital experience.
- Low Trust, Low Friction: Active Churn. They are already leaving for a Fintech.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Predatory Perception' Multiplier:** Feedback explicitly using words like "scam," "theft," or "trap" to describe a standard bank policy (like a maintenance fee) is weighted at 5x. When the customer views the bank as an active adversary stealing their money, no amount of positive marketing can repair the relationship.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The "Utility vs Advisor" Base:** The Analytics agent must determine how the user views the bank. Are they a "Dumb Pipe" (I just store money here so I can use Apple Pay) or a "Financial Advisor" (I rely on them to help me buy a house)? Legacy banks desperately want to be Advisors, but consumers increasingly view them as Dumb Pipes. The Analytics report must expose this gap.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Institution evaluated, specific product/fee in question.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Trust Matrix (e.g., "A Portfolio of Hostage Loyalty").
**Section 3: The 'Friction Anchor' Audit**
Specific measurement of exactly *why* users haven't left yet (e.g., direct deposit tied to employer).
**Section 4: The Fee Resentment Log**
Granular analysis of which specific fees (overdraft, wire, minimum balance) trigger the highest emotional damage.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the fee structure changes, UI overhauls, or API integration changes requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Fintech Bleed." If transcripts show that users keep their legacy checking account for their salary, but instantly transfer 80% of their spending money to Venmo/CashApp/Robinhood to manage their daily lives, the Analytics agent must flag that the legacy bank has already lost the daily relationship with the customer.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Hostage Loyalty' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the trust data reveals a terminal reliance on 'Hostage Loyalty' within the millennial demographic. While the institution's retention rate looks mathematically healthy, qualitative behavioral data shows that [Percentage]% of these accountholders actively despise the institution due to hidden maintenance fees. They are remaining explicitly because they believe migrating their auto-pay ecosystem is too difficult. We strongly advise the institution to view this entire cohort as a flight-risk. The moment a Fintech competitor achieves a seamless '1-Click' account migration tool, this demographic will churn immediately. Retention is currently artificial."
