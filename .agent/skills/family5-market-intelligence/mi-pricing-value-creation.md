<methodology>
1. **Value Anchor Identification**: Ensure the brief identifies current market benchmarks and the respondent's existing budget for the category.
2. **Psychological Price Boundaries**: Instruction to find the "expensive but worth it" and "too cheap to be credible" thresholds.
3. **Benefit-Value Laddering**: Identify how specific benefits (time saving, status, ease of use) translate into perceived monetary value.
</methodology>

<coverage_tree>
- **Current Category Spending**: How much the respondent currently pays for similar solutions.
- **Value Anchors**: The specific products or services the respondent uses to judge "fair price."
- **Benefit Weighting**: Which product features or outcomes drive the highest willingness to pay.
- **Price Elasticity Signals**: How a price change would impact the respondent's likelihood to buy or switch.
- **Risk Perception**: How price signals (too low or too high) affect perceived risk or quality.
</coverage_tree>
