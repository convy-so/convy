## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Audit the psychological map of friction. Identify the "Safe Pricing Corridor" where value outstrips cost.
**Key Metric:** **Skin in the Game Weighting.** Weight feedback from respondents who bought a premium competitor in the last 6 months at 3x.
**Principle:** **The Reference Anchor.** All reactions MUST be evaluated against the "Current Spend" cited in the warmup.
**Quality Rule:** Distinguish between "Price Resistance" (Too expensive) and "Value Failure" (Irrelevant at any price).

## Critical Misreadings
- **The Discount Trap:** Interpreting "I'd buy it if it's 50% off" as a pricing signal rather than a lack of core utility.
- **Hypothetical Inflation:** Taking stated WTP as literal quantitative demand rather than a marker of resistance.
- **Skepticism Floor:** Ignoring when a price is "Too Cheap," causing a loss of trust/quality signaling.
- **Segment Blindness:** Averaging price sensitivity across disparate segments (SMB vs. Enterprise).

## Threshold Analysis Matrix
| Barrier | Signal | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Marginal Resistance | Grumbling but paying | Boundary of current loyalty. |
| Extreme Resistance | Instant Churn / Cancellation | Absolute pricing ceiling. |
| Untrustworthy Floor | "Too cheap to be good" | Quality floor; pricing below this destroys trust. |
| The "Veblen" Effect | Rejecting lower prices | Price IS the feature; establishments status/security. |

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Monetized Feature | High WTP for one specific item | Roadmap Priority: Pull this feature out as a paid add-on. |
| Model Friction | Hatred of specific sub models | Packaging Pivot: Move from Flat to Tiered or Usage-based. |
| Anchor Contrast | High sensitivity vs. low-cost rival| Messaging Pivot: Focus on "Risk Premium" (Section 7). |
| Principle Churn | "Rip-off" mentions | Sentiment Warning: Brand damage risk if hike proceeds. |

## Synthesis Protocols
- **Step 1 (The Threshold Audit):** Map the Marginal vs. Extreme resistance points.
- **Step 2 (The Benefit Weighting):** Isolate features that justified the "Pricing Gun" upsell.
- **Step 3 (The Anchor Audit):** Link price reactions to the respondent's current category spend.
- **Step 4 (The Model Audit):** Evaluate reaction to the Pricing Perimeter (Sub vs. One-off).

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Study Parameters** (Pricing models, Audience segments, Competitive anchors).
**Part 2: Executive Summary** (Defining the Safe Pricing Corridor and primary value driver).
**Part 3: The Value Equation** (Monetized features vs. expected standards).
**Part 4: Threshold Analysis** (Psychological barriers, floors, and ceilings).
**Part 5: Decision Map Response** (Tiering strategies, promo plans, or hike communications).

## Flagging Templates
**HYPOTHETICAL BIAS WARNING:**
> "Stated preferences carry artificial suppression bias. Reported thresholds are psychological resistance markers, not quantitative demand forecasts. Validate via live market A/B test."

**TIERING MANDATE:**
> "High fragmentation in elasticity (SMB vs ENTERPRISE). A single horizontal price is not viable; immediate tiered pricing structure required to avoid mass churn or missed revenue."

**VALUE FAILURE:**
> "Respondents only accept the product at 'Almost Free' levels. This is a core utility failure, not a pricing issue. Proposition Testing required."
