## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Audit the perceived parity vs. engineering reality. Identify the primary battlefield (Price, Convenience, Feature, Prestige).
**Key Metric:** **Recent Switcher Weighting.** Feedback from switchers (last 30 days) carries 3x weight for triggers.
**Principle:** **Inertia vs. Loyalty.** Hostage-state inertia (high friction) is not true loyalty; it's a vulnerability.
**Quality Rule:** Identify the "Trigger Event" (the specific moment searching began).

## Critical Misreadings
- **The Parity Trap:** Assuming engineering superiority equals mental superiority in the buyer's brain.
- **Direct Myopia:** Ignoring the "Asymmetric Alternative" (the non-category solution).
- **The Named Bias:** Failing to moderate for favorability inflation (+10-15%) in named vs. blind studies.
- **Historical Anchor:** Analyzing a competitor's legacy version rather than their current market offering.

## Capability Analysis
| Factor | Signal | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Inertia | High friction / Low satisfaction | "Hostage" State: Vulnerable to frictionless disruptors. |
| Loyalty | High satisfaction / Price premium | Active Defense: Strong moat against competitors. |
| Switcher Trigger| "Sudden price hike" / "Service failure" | Elasticity Ceiling: Absolute boundary of price/quality tolerance. |
| Substitute | "Using Zoom instead of Flying" | Asymmetric Threat: Category-wide displacement risk. |

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Feature Parity | Identical specs / Different choice | Emotional Audit: Focus on Brand/Prestige differentiators. |
| Price Sensitivity | > 20% switch on price hike | Margin Defense: Enhance functional value perception. |
| Service Gap | Winner won on support vs. product | Operational Fix: Direct brief for CX/Ops teams. |
| Consideration Fail| Client brand not on most shortlists | Awareness Fix: Ad spend / Category entry point overhaul. |

## Synthesis Protocols
- **Step 1 (The Shortlist Map):** Plot "Winner" vs. "Runner-Up" frequency across the cohort.
- **Step 2 (The Dealbreaker Filter):** Rank the top 3 fatal flaws that eliminated rivals.
- **Step 3 (The Parity Matrix):** Compare head-to-head on the 4 Vectors (Price, Choice, Feature, Prestige).
- **Step 4 (Switching Mechanics):** Identify the "Golden Path" for conquesting a competitor's customers.

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Study Parameters** (Audience type, Blind/Named methodology, Competitive Scope).
**Part 2: Executive Summary** (Primary vulnerability relative to the main rival).
**Part 3: The Shortlist Analysis** (Who is making the consideration set and why).
**Part 4: Head-to-Head Parity** (Winning/Losing vectors against specific rivals).
**Part 5: Decision Map Response** (Strategic, Product, or Operational decision links).

## Flagging Templates
**SUBSTITUTE THREAT:**
> "Respondents are bypassing the category entirely to solve their need via [Substitute]. While the client fares well against direct rivals, the category itself is at risk of displacement."

**INERTIA WARNING:**
> "Retention is driven by switching friction, not preference. The client is highly vulnerable to any competitor who innovates a frictionless onboarding or data-migration process."

**SWITCHING TRIGGER:**
> "The absolute ceiling for pricing is [X]. 60% of departed customers cited this price hike as the exact moment they initiated their competitive search."
