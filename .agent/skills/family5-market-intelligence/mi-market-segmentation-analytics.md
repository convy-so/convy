## Interpretation Framework
**Goal:** Audit behavioral patterns across cohorts. Identify the "Wetware" (shared anxiety/aspiration) regardless of demographics.
**Key Metric:** **Aspirational Discounting.** Discount self-reported traits (Sustainability/Ethics) if they contradict reported behavior (Price/Habit).
**Principle:** **Needs vs. Behaviors.** Same Need + Different Behavior = Different Segment (driven by constraint).
**Quality Rule:** Defined clusters must describe a distinct "Job to be Done" with unique priorities.

## Critical Misreadings
- **The Demographic Trap:** Reporting based on age/gender/zip code rather than behavioral drivers.
- **Overlap Confusion:** Failing to distinguish segments when they share a single feature priority.
- **Aspirational Error:** Building a segment profile on what the respondent *wants* to be, rather than what they are.
- **Context Blindness:** Ignoring the "Macro Life Context" that governs the purchase window.

## Segment Analysis Matrix
| Factor | Signal | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Constraint | Same Need / Diff. Behavior | Segments split by Price, Time, or Skill level. |
| Confusion | Diff. Need / Same Behavior | Overlapping audiences; potential for messaging drift. |
| Catalyst | Shared breakage/trigger event | Momentum-based Segment (e.g., New Parents, Job Change). |
| Off-Label | Cluster using product "wrongly" | High-Growth Opportunity: Unserved Shadow Segment. |

## Synthesis & Reporting Patterns
| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Dominant Anchor | 60%+ share same primary motive | Core Segment Focus: Double down on the primary driver. |
| Divergent Needs | Pro (Complexity) vs. Novice (Ease)| Expansion Risk: Avoid product bloat; split the UX/UI. |
| Low Priority | Item #1 to cut from budget | Defensive Priority: Focus on utility/necessity messaging. |
| Fragmentation | No clear clusters found | Quant. Request: Broad survey needed to find scale. |

## Synthesis Protocols
- **Step 1 (The Cluster Map):** Group by shared "Breaking Points" (Catalysts).
- **Step 2 (The Behavioral Filter):** Separate "Aspirational Self" claims from "Usage Reality."
- **Step 3 (The Priority Rank):** Map which segments consider the product an "Untouchable Necessity."
- **Step 4 (The Growth Path):** Identify the cohort with the highest unmet need/greatest 'hack' frequency.

## Reporting Artifacts
**Part 1: Study Parameters** (Audience scope, Validation/Discovery mandate, Category context).
**Part 2: Executive Summary** (Defining the segment with the highest growth potential).
**Part 3: Segment Profile Matrix** (JTBD, Core Values, Feature Priorities, and Price Sensitivity).
**Part 4: The Underserved Gap** (Analyzing 'hacks' and unserved workarounds).
**Part 5: Decision Map Response** (Strategic, Product, or Marketing targeting links).

## Flagging Templates
**FRAGMENTATION WARNING:**
> "The cohort did not cluster into dominant segments. Data reveals Idiosyncratic Use-Cases. Recommend quantitative sizing to determine if any micro-segment justifies a dedicated roadmap."

**OFF-LABEL OPPORTUNITY:**
> "A significant sub-segment is utilizing the product for [Off-Label Use] to solve [Unserved Need]. This represents an organic growth opportunity outside current brand positioning."

**ASPIRATIONAL CONTRADICTION:**
> "Respondent claims [Aspirational Trait] as a driver, but behavioral data (Price focus/Habit) contradicts this. Profile weighted toward actual utility behavior."
