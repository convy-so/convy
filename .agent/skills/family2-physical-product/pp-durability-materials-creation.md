<methodology>
1. **Quality Cues**: Ensure the brief identifies the specific physical signals of quality (Weight, Texture, Sound, Finish).
2. **Longevity Expectations**: Instruction to define what "sturdy" or "built to last" means in the product's category.
3. **Point-of-Purchase vs. Use-Experience**: Distinguish between how quality is perceived at first sight versus after a period of usage.
</methodology>

<coverage_tree>
- **Initial Durability Impression**: First reaction to the product's build quality.
- **Material Selection**: Satisfaction with the choices of metal, plastic, fabric, or wood.
- **Surface Finish**: Perception of the quality and durability of the coating or paint.
- **Mechanical Trust**: Confidence in moving parts, buttons, and hinges.
- **Wear & Tear Projections**: How the respondent imagines the product will look after 1-2 years of use.
</coverage_tree>
