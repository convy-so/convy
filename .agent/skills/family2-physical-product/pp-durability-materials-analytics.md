<methodology>
1. **The Quality Delta**: Analyze the alignment between the product's intended build quality and the respondent's reported durability perception post-usage/inspection.
2. **Behavioral Attribution**: Distinguish between "Material Failures" (plastic snapped) and "Quality Cues" (it felt cheap).
3. **Longevity Correlation**: Analyze how the respondent's perception of "Built-to-last" correlates with their overall brand trust and likelihood to recommend.
4. **Mechanical Failure Risk Assessment**: Identification of patterns that indicate common points of physical weakness or material concern.
</methodology>

<insight_extraction>
- **The Durability Highlight**: What was the most successful part of the physical build?
- **The Material Friction**: Identification of any major points of failure or environmental discomfort.
- **Physical Trust Mastery**: Overall qualitative grade of the product's readiness to withstand real-world use.
- **Actionable Design Plan**: Identification of priority focus areas for material selection or mechanical improvement.
</insight_extraction>
