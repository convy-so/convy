# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Packaging data determines the emotional state of the customer the moment they first touch the product. If the packaging is deeply frustrating ("wrap rage"), the customer begins using the product in a state of high annoyance. If the packaging feels cheap, the customer immediately questions if they overpaid for the product inside. The Analytics agent must synthesize the structural opening-mechanics with the psychological brand-perception to determine if the box elevated or damaged the product experience.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Unboxing Matrix:**
- High Friction, Low Premium: The worst-case scenario. It's hard to open and looks cheap. (e.g., standard plastic clamshells).
- High Friction, High Premium: The "Fortress of Luxury." Very well protected, high perceived value, but annoying to breach.
- Low Friction, Low Premium: Standard commodity. Easy to open, completely forgettable (e.g., a brown corrugated box).
- Low Friction, High Premium: The "Apple Box." Flawless, smooth reveal. The gold standard.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Damage Risk' Multiplier:** If the respondent describes a structural opening mechanism that requires them to apply excessive force (which could result in them shooting the product out of the box or stabbing it with a knife), this is weighted at 5x. It is an immediate product-liability and returns-risk issue.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The Sustainability Threshold:** The Analytics agent must index the target audience's core values against the packaging. If the product is marketed as a "Green/Earth-Friendly" solution, but respondents consistently flag the heavy use of single-use plastics in the box, the brand authenticity is broken.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Format tested (Retail vs D2C), Primary objective tested.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the packaging's ability to elevate the enclosed product.
**Section 3: The Mechanical Friction Report**
A step-by-step unboxing analysis identifying specifically where the user struggled.
**Section 4: The Perceived Value Delta**
Calculation demonstrating whether the packaging makes the product feel more or less expensive than it is.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the budget reallocation, material shifts, or redesigns requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for the "Information Void." If the packaging is overly minimalist (to look premium) but results in 40% of users not understanding what the product actually does before they open it, the Analytics agent must flag the design team for highly damaging over-indexing on aesthetics at the cost of utility.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When wrap-rage poses a physical threat to the product:**
"Synthesis of the unboxing protocols reveals a severe mechanical failure in the internal retention tray. [Percentage]% of respondents noted that the force required to break the product free from the mold resulted in a sudden, violent release, frequently causing the product to be dropped or forcefully impacted immediately upon opening. This design flaw introduces a massive liability for Day-1 breakage returns. We strongly advise redesigning the internal tray with deeper thumb-notches or a softer retention material."
