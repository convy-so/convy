# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Vendor Evaluation data is the battlefield report. It strips away a company's internal delusions about their own product. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify why the client is actually winning or losing. If the client thinks they win because of their "World-Class AI," but the Analytics agent discovers they actually win because "They are 10% cheaper than the legacy option," the Analytics agent must forcibly reset the client's entire Go-To-Market strategy. The "Tie-Breaker" is the most valuable metric in B2B software.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Competitive Matrix:**
- High Win Rate, Differentiated Product: The Market Leader. Price on value.
- High Win Rate, Commoditized Product: The Sales/Marketing Machine. The product is average, but the distribution is elite. Highly vulnerable to a cheaper copycat.
- Low Win Rate, Differentiated Product: The Strategy Failure. The product is great, but the sales team can't communicate the value, or the pricing structure is broken.
- Low Win Rate, Commoditized Product: The Dying Incumbent. Pivot or perish.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Tie-Breaker' Multiplier:** Feedback that isolates the exact, absolute final reason a deal swung left or right (e.g., "They had a native integration with our obscure accounting tool") is weighted at 5x. This data directly writes the product roadmap for the next quarter.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The 'Commodity' Check:** The Analytics agent must aggressively scan the transcripts for words like "standard," "baseline," or "table stakes." If the client's flagship marketing feature is viewed by the cohort as "table stakes" (meaning every competitor also has it), the Analytics agent must explicitly instruct the client to stop dedicating ad spend to that feature.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Win/Loss scope, primary competitors evaluated.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the client's Competitive Posture (e.g., Market Leader vs Dying Incumbent).
**Section 3: The 'Tie-Breaker' Audit**
Prioritized ranking of the exact features, prices, or sales tactics that decided the final outcome.
**Section 4: The 'Commodity' vs 'Differentiator' Map**
A brutal classification of the client's feature set as viewed by the actual market.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the pricing changes, sales playbook updates, or product roadmap shifts requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt) Effectiveness." If 60% of lost deals note that the competitor successfully convinced the buyer that the client's software "isn't secure enough," the Analytics agent must instruct the marketing team to build a dedicated "Security Trust Center" defensive campaign immediately.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When a 'Commodity Delusion' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the competitive transcripts reveals a severe 'Commodity Delusion' within the client's Go-To-Market strategy. Across [Percentage]% of the evaluations, buyers explicitly stated that [Core Flagship Feature] is now viewed as standard 'table stakes' across all top 3 vendors. The client is losing deals because they are spending 50% of their sales pitch highlighting a feature the market expects by default, while [Competitor Name] is spending that time highlighting their localized support. We strongly advise immediately retiring [Feature Name] from top-line marketing positioning and pivoting entirely to [Newly Identified Differentiator]."
