# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Channel data is an audit of the "Path of Least Resistance." A reseller has 50 products they could pitch on any given phone call. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to determine exactly where the client's product ranks in that hierarchy, and why. If the product has the best margins in the industry but the worst Deal Registration portal, the Analytics agent must calculate whether the administrative friction is outweighing the financial reward. Furthermore, the agent must aggressively hunt for the cancer of the channel: "Channel Conflict" (distrust between the reseller and the client's direct sales team).

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Channel Matrix:**
- High Margin, Low Friction: The Flagship Product. Resellers will actively push this over competitors.
- Low Margin, Low Friction: The Easy Add-On. Resellers will tack it onto deals, but won't lead with it.
- High Margin, High Friction: The Frustrating Sell. Resellers complain constantly but sell it anyway. Requires immediate operational streamlining.
- Low Margin, High Friction: Dead Inventory. Resellers will actively avoid mentioning it.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Channel Conflict' Multiplier:** Feedback indicating that the reseller believes the client is actively competing against them or stealing their registered leads is weighted at 5x. Distrust in deal protection will cause a mass exodus of top-tier partners.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The "Collateral Usage" Baseline:** The Analytics agent must heavily discount positive feedback about the partner portal if the behavioral data shows the resellers never log into it. "The brochures look nice" is useless data if the reps are building their own slide decks.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Partner tiers evaluated, primary competitor baselines.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Channel Hierarchy (Flagship vs Dead Inventory).
**Section 3: The 'Channel Conflict' Audit**
Severity ranking of trust issues regarding lead protection and direct-sales overlap.
**Section 4: The 'Margin vs Friction' Analysis**
Calculation of whether the financial incentives are sufficient given the operational difficulty of quoting/selling.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the portal redesigns, PRM changes, or rules-of-engagement updates requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for the "Bait and Switch." If 40% of resellers admit that they only register deals to stay in the program's 'Gold Tier,' but actively suggest competitor products to the client behind closed doors to save their own support margins, the Analytics agent must alert the company to massive systemic channel fraud.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Channel Conflict' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the Deal Registration data reveals a terminal breach of trust across the mid-market partner tier. [Percentage]% of respondents explicitly cited instances where they believe [Client's] Direct Sales team leveraged portal data to poach high-value accounts. Consequently, these partners are now actively withholding their pipeline data and refusing to register deals until the contract is already signed. This 'Channel Conflict' renders the revenue forecasting model entirely invalid. We strongly advise an immediate executive intervention and a public rewrite of the 'Rules of Engagement' to guarantee strict channel protection."
