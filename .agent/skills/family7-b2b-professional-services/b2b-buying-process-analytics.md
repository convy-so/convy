# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
B2B Buying data writes the "Sales Playbook." If a company's sales reps are pitching the software to Marketing Directors, but the Analytics agent discovers that the CFO *always* vetoes the deal at the last minute because there is no hard financial ROI, the sales strategy is fundamentally broken. The Analytics agent must synthesize these interviews to build an "Internal Map" of the buyer's organization. It tells the client *who* to sell to, *what* to give them to get past Legal, and *when* to expect the deal to actually close.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Procurement Matrix:**
- Champion-Led, Fast Close: The ideal state. High product desire, low bureaucratic friction.
- Executive-Mandated, Slow Close: A top-down purchase dragged out by middle-management resistance or IT security.
- Champion-Led, Vetoed: The tragedy. The team wants it, but Legal/IT/Finance killed it. Identify the specific veto reason immediately.
- Committee Consensus: A slow, painful process requiring 5+ stakeholders to agree. Requires massive sales-enablement material.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Veto' Multiplier:** Feedback identifying the exact moment or reason a deal was delayed or almost killed by a non-user (e.g., Legal, IT, Compliance) is weighted at 4x. Sales reps are usually blind to this internal friction; the research exists to illuminate it.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The 'True Cycle' Baseline:** The Analytics agent must compare the "True Sales Cycle" (from the buyer's initial problem recognition) to the "CRM Sales Cycle" (from the moment the lead entered the client's system). If buyers research for 6 months before calling sales, the client must invest heavily in top-of-funnel content to influence that silent research period.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Win/Loss scope, deal sizes, respondent personas (Champion vs Buyer).
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the primary bottleneck or accelerator in the sales cycle.
**Section 3: The Committee & Veto Map**
A prioritized list of the non-obvious stakeholders required to close the deal, and what their specific fears are.
**Section 4: The 'Procurement Black Hole' Diagnostic**
Analysis of legal, security, and implementation friction.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the sales playbook updates, contract simplifications, or ROI tools requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for the "Universal Objection." If 60% of the lost deals died specifically during the IT Security review because the client lacked a specific compliance certification, the Analytics agent must flag this as an existential barrier to revenue growth and recommend immediate engineering investment in that certification.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When a systemic 'Hidden Veto' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the procurement timelines reveals a severe and systemic bottleneck in the late-stage sales cycle. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated deals, the initial 'Champion' successfully secured team buy-in, but the deal was effectively halted by the [Specific Department - e.g., Information Security] during the final review phase. This is occurring because the current sales enablement material fails to pre-emptively address [Specific Security/Compliance Concern]. We strongly advise the Sales Ops team to develop a dedicated "Security Info-Pack" to be distributed to the Champion at the start of the evaluation, bypassing the late-stage veto."
