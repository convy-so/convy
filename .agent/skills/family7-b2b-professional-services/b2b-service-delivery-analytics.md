# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Service Delivery data is the early warning system for enterprise churn. If a customer is angry about a specific feature, they might wait for a patch. If a customer is treated badly by the support team during a crisis, they will churn immediately upon renewal. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify structural failures in the company's operating model: Are sales reps fundamentally lying to close deals? Is onboarding structurally under-resourced? Are CSMs acting as strategic advisors, or just expensive call-center agents?

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Delivery Matrix:**
- Fast TTV, High proactive Support: The "Sticky" ideal. Maximum retention.
- Slow TTV, High proactive Support: Forgiven Friction. Onboarding sucked, but the team held their hand through it so trust remains.
- Fast TTV, Low Support: The Defensive Posture. It works, but the client feels ignored. One major bug away from churning.
- Slow TTV, Low Support: The "Dead on Arrival" cohort. They will churn before year one is over. Intervene immediately.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Orphan Handoff' Multiplier:** Feedback indicating that the client felt abandoned or had to "re-explain" their business during the transition from Sales to Support/Implementation is weighted at 4x. This is the most critical juncture in the customer lifecycle and the easiest to fix structurally.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The 'Sales vs Reality' Gap:** The Analytics agent must aggressively compare the stated "Sales Promise" against the "Execution Reality." If 100% of deals closed because sales promised a 30-day implementation, but the data shows the average is 90 days, the Analytics agent must explicitly instruct the client to change their sales quotas, as the current model is generating guaranteed, structurally baked-in churn.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Support tiers evaluated, expected Time-to-Value baseline.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Service Delivery health (e.g., Identification of the 'Sales vs Reality' gap severity).
**Section 3: The Onboarding Autopsy**
Chronological breakdown of exactly where the implementation timeline breaks down.
**Section 4: CSM & Support Efficacy Audit**
Evaluation of whether the Account Management team is viewed as strategic advisors or reactive ticket-routers.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the onboarding process overhauls, CSM KPI changes, or support capacity increases requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for the "Bypass Protocol." If high-value respondents universally mention that they ignore standard support channels and text their CSM directly to get things done, the Analytics agent must flag that the standard support infrastructure is viewed as entirely broken and is being bypassed via personal capital.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When a systemic 'Sales vs Reality' gap is detected:**
"Synthesis of the implementation data reveals a terminal misalignment between the Sales Organization's commitments and the Operations Organization's capabilities. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated accounts, respondents explicitly cited that they were promised a 'turn-key' deployment within [X] days, whereas behavioral tracing shows actual deployment required severe manual intervention by the client over [Y] days. This gap is not a support failure; it is an expectation-setting failure originating in the pre-sale cycle. We strongly advise pausing all 'Speed-to-Launch' marketing messaging until operational capacity can meet the baseline promises being made by account executives."
