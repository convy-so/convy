# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Partnership data tells a company who their real friends are. Most B2B partnerships decay into "zombie" states—they still exist on a website footer, but generate zero revenue. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify whether a partnership is a True Alliance (mutual value), a Resource Drain (costs more than it makes), or a Hostage Situation (one side is trapped). The data dictates whether the client should invest millions into deepening the technical integration, or quietly kill the partnership to save operational bandwidth.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Partnership Matrix:**
- High Strategic Alignment, High Operational Execution (The True Alliance): Scale this immediately.
- High Strategic Alignment, Low Operational Execution (The Friction Trap): Executives love it, middle-management is failing to execute. Requires immediate operational overhaul.
- Low Strategic Alignment, High Operational Execution (The Tactical Vendor): They aren't a partner, they are a glorified vendor. Stop treating them like a strategic ally.
- Low Strategic Alignment, Low Operational Execution (The Barney Partnership): Exists in name only. Dissolve or downgrade.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Asymmetry' Multiplier:** Feedback regarding asynchronous effort (e.g., "We sent them 40 leads this quarter, they sent us 2") is weighted at 4x. Mathematical asymmetry is the leading indicator of partnership death. Once a partner realizes they are being exploited, trust cannot be recovered.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The Executive/Operator Disconnect:** The Analytics agent must explicitly contrast the sentiment of Partner Executives vs Partner Operators. If the CEO says the partnership is "Transformative" but the Engineers say the API is "A liability," the Analytics agent must weight the Operator's reality higher for short-term health, and the Executive's for long-term survival.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Partnership types evaluated, respondent tiers (Exec vs Operator).
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the portfolio (e.g., Identifying the Barney Partnerships).
**Section 3: The Asymmetry Report**
Detailed mapping of who is doing the "heavy lifting" across marketing, sales, and tech.
**Section 4: Operational Friction Points**
The specific communication and bureaucratic barriers stalling joint revenue.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the tier-downgrades, operational syncs, or resource allocations requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for the "Brand Vampire." If multiple partners explicitly state that they only tolerate the client's terrible operational support because they desperately need to put the client's logo on their website, the Analytics agent must flag this. The partnership is sustained entirely by Brand Equity, which will eventually collapse if operational execution doesn't improve.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When a 'Barney Partnership' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the execution data reveals that the alliance with [Partner Name] functions almost entirely as a 'Barney Partnership' (existing in press releases but generating statistically zero joint value). While Strategic Alignment scores remain superficially high among the executive tier, the Operational Execution score is effectively zero. [Percentage]% of operational respondents noted that neither side initiates joint go-to-market motions due to misaligned incentive structures. We strongly advise downgrading this partner to a 'Self-Serve' tier, removing dedicated Partner Success resources to stop the operational bleed."
