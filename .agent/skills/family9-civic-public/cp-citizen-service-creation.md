---
name: Citizen Service Experience (Creation)
description: Briefing and methodology for public service accessibility and satisfaction level research.
id: cp-citizen-service-creation
---

<methodology>
1. **Public-Realm Access Mapping**: Ensure the brief identifies the "Delta" between the service's intended accessibility and the citizen's reported barriers.
2. **Inclusivity & Barrier Audit**: Instruction to evaluate the perception of the service's welcoming and its impact on the energy of the community interaction.
3. **Public Hero Identification**: Determine if there are specific public servants or behaviors that either encouraged or discouraged the citizen's trust in the service.
</methodology>

<coverage_tree>
- **Initial Impression**: First word or feeling that comes to mind when the public service is mentioned.
- **Service Accessibility**: Ease of locating, contacting, and using the public service.
- **Service Efficacy**: How well the service is perceived to meet the business/citizen needs.
- **Institutional Trust**: Relationship with the service team and feeling of being valued as a citizen.
- **Public Policy Alignment**: perception of the service's ability to support the company's/community's core mission and goals.
- **Brand Reputation**: How the public service represents the government/institution in the community.
- **Service Persistence Likelihood**: Likelihood of the citizen to continue using the service or recommending it to others.
</coverage_tree>
