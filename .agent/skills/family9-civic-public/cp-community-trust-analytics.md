# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Community Trust data is the existential health metric of a municipality. If this metric drops too low, policies cannot be executed, public health initiatives fail (because no one believes the warnings), and civic participation collapses. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify whether a lack of trust is localized (e.g., "We hate the Parks Department") or systemic (e.g., "The entire City Hall is corrupt"). The agent must ruthlessly expose the "Fairness Gap"—the measurable difference between how the government thinks it is distributing resources versus how the citizens perceive that distribution.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Trust Matrix:**
- High Trust, High Competence Perceived: The Civic Mandate. The government has the political capital to execute massive, disruptive projects.
- High Trust, Low Competence Perceived: The "Good Intentions" Trap. The community likes the leadership personally, but thinks they are in over their heads.
- Low Trust, High Competence Perceived: The "Evil Genius" Paradigm. The community thinks the government is brilliantly enriching its own donors. High danger of civic unrest.
- Low Trust, Low Competence Perceived: Complete Institutional Collapse. Nobody votes, nobody complies.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Historical Grievance' Multiplier:** Feedback that links a current lack of trust to a specific historical failure (e.g., "I don't trust the water safety board because they lied to us in 2014") is weighted at 4x. This indicates that the trust deficit is entrenched in the community's cultural memory and will take a massive, structural apology to fix.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The Partisan Filter:** The Analytics agent must differentiate between "Partisan Disagreement" (I didn't vote for them, so I don't trust their tax plan) and "Institutional Rot" (I voted for them, but they still take bribes). "Institutional Rot" is a vastly more dangerous metric and must be highlighted in the Executive Summary.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Specific institution evaluated, demographic/geographic bounds.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Civic Mandate (e.g., "Institutional Collapse in the South Ward").
**Section 3: The 'Fairness Gap' Report**
A geographical or demographic mapping of who feels favored vs who feels ignored.
**Section 4: The Transparency & Corruption Audit**
Analysis of whether the community believes data published by the government.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the independent audits, budget reallocations, or town halls requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for "Geographic Resentment." If transcripts from Zip Code A show 90% trust, and transcripts from Zip Code B show 10% trust, the Analytics agent must flag this as a "Two Cities" crisis. Averages are useless here; the agent must highlight the variance.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When severe 'Institutional Rot' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the trust data reveals a complete collapse of institutional credibility regarding the [Specific Department]. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated citizens, responses indicated a deeply entrenched belief that the department operates with intentional malice or hidden financial agendas favoring local developers. This level of systemic cynicism renders standard PR and marketing campaigns entirely useless. We strongly advise pausing all 'Good News' municipal messaging regarding this department; the community views positive marketing as gaslighting. Trust recovery will explicitly require an independent, third-party audit and a public acknowledgment of historical failure."
