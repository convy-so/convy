# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Voter Sentiment data is the raw emotional telemetry of an electorate. Campaigns burn millions of dollars trying to move an electorate that is often unmovable. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify the "Voter Reality." If a campaign is spending 80% of its budget talking about "The Environment," but the Analytics agent discovers the Swing Voters only care about "Crime," the campaign will lose. The agent must ruthlessly expose the "Shy Voter" effect and the "Enthusiasm Gap" to prevent the client from believing false-positive polling data.

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Electoral Matrix:**
- High Alignment, High Enthusiasm: The Base. They will vote no matter what.
- High Alignment, Low Enthusiasm: The Liability. They agree with the candidate, but won't bother voting. The campaign must switch from "Persuasion" to "Mobilization/Fear."
- Low Alignment, High Enthusiasm: The Opposition. They are highly motivated to defeat the candidate. Do not engage.
- Low Alignment, Low Enthusiasm: The True Undecided. The ultimate battleground. They can be won with hyper-local, practical messaging.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Dealbreaker' Multiplier:** Feedback that explicitly names a single issue as overriding all other political affiliations (e.g., "I'm a lifelong Democrat, but I will vote Republican just to stop this specific school rezoning plan") is weighted at 5x. This is the structural fracture point of a voting bloc.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The 'Anti-Vote' Adjustment:** The Analytics agent must heavily adjust predictive outcomes based on "Anti-Voting." If 70% of a candidate's supporters explicitly state they don't actually like the candidate, but are just voting *against* the opponent, the Analytics agent must flag this coalition as extremely fragile and highly susceptible to third-party defectors or stay-at-home apathy.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Candidate/Issue evaluated, demographic/geographic bounds.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Electoral Reality (e.g., "High Alignment, Terminal Enthusiasm Gap").
**Section 3: The 'Dealbreaker' Matrix**
Prioritized mapping of the specific issues that are overriding traditional party loyalty.
**Section 4: Attack Narrative Susceptibility**
Analysis of which opposition attacks are actually working vs which are backfiring.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the ad buys, messaging pivots, or debate prep strategies requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Echo Chamber Deviation." If the aggregated transcripts from actual voters show completely different priorities than the talking points dominating Twitter/Cable News, the Analytics agent must explicitly instruct the campaign team to log off social media and return to localized messaging.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When a terminal 'Enthusiasm Gap' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the voter sentiment data reveals a terminal 'Enthusiasm Gap' within the core demographic of [Demographic Name]. While traditional polling may show [Candidate] winning this demographic by +10 points, the qualitative behavioral data indicates that [Percentage]% of these supporters are 'Low-Conviction / High-Apathy'. They agree with the platform, but express zero motivation to actually cast a ballot, citing a lack of inspiring leadership. If the election were held tomorrow beneath adverse conditions (e.g., bad weather, long lines), this model predicts a massive turnout failure. We strongly advise pausing all 'Persuasion' ad spending intended to win new voters, and reallocating the entire budget to 'Mobilization and Base-Fired' messaging (fear of opponent) to ensure the current base actually shows up."
