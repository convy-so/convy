# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Policy Perception data is the autopsy of a communications strategy. A policy is only as effective as the public's willingness to comply with it. The Analytics agent must synthesize the transcripts to identify "The Narrative Gap." If a policy was designed to save the middle class money, but the middle class believes it will bankrupt them, the policy is effectively a political failure regardless of the math. The Analytics agent must identify exactly *what* the public believes, *why* they believe it, and *who* convinced them. 

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Policy Matrix:**
- High Comprehension, High Alignment: The Civic Win. The public gets it and likes it. Proceed with rollout.
- Low Comprehension, High Alignment: Blind Faith. They don't know how it works, but they trust the goal. Highly vulnerable to future misinformation.
- High Comprehension, Low Alignment: Ideological Warfare. They understand exactly what the policy does, and they hate it. This cannot be solved with "better messaging"; it requires changing the policy.
- Low Comprehension, Low Alignment: The PR Disaster. A toxic mix of confusion and fear. Rebrand and re-educate.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Method vs Goal' Multiplier:** Feedback that explicitly differentiates between supporting the *goal* of a policy (e.g., reducing traffic) while opposing the *method* (e.g., toll roads) is weighted at 4x. This proves that ideological resistance is not absolute and offers a pathway for policy compromise.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The Fact-Check Baseline:** The Analytics agent must explicitly contrast what the respondents *believe* the policy does against the factual reality established in the Brief. If there is a massive delta, the primary finding is not "opposition," but "communication failure."

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Specific policy evaluated, demographic/geographic bounds.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Narrative Gap (e.g., "The PR Disaster").
**Section 3: The Comprehension vs Reality Audit**
A list of the top 3 most pervasive public misunderstandings regarding the actual mechanics of the law.
**Section 4: The Misinformation Origin Vector**
Identification of where the opposition narrative is coming from (local news, specific social media platforms, word-of-mouth).
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the targeted ad campaigns, message pivots, or policy adjustments requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze the "Enforceability Assumption." If multiple transcripts show that citizens actually support a new punitive tax or fine in theory, but universally believe "the city will never actually enforce it on rich people," the Analytics agent must flag an underlying crisis of institutional enforcement credibility.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When a severe 'Narrative Gap' is detected:**
"Synthesis of the perception data reveals a terminal 'Narrative Gap' undermining the rollout of [Policy Name]. Across [Percentage]% of evaluated citizens, there is a hardened belief that the policy's primary mechanism involves [Specific Misinformation], which is factually incorrect. This narrative is primarily being driven by [Identified Origin Vector, e.g., local Facebook groups], entirely bypassing official city communications. We strongly advise pausing all policy defense messaging; arguing the merits of the policy is currently useless because the public and the city are arguing about fundamentally different assumed facts. An aggressive, simplified 'Myth vs Fact' re-education campaign must precede any further policy defense."
