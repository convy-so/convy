# Section 1: What This Domain's Data Actually Means
Technology Adoption data dictates timing and messaging. It tells a company whether they need to build an educational bridge for a terrified market, or if they need to build a life-boat because the market views the technology as inevitable. The Analytics agent must synthesize the existential fears of the cohort into actionable product positioning. If the market views an AI product as a "job killer," marketing it as "highly efficient" will fail; it must be marketed as an "assistant that makes you faster."

# Section 2: Coverage Interpretation Guide
**The Adoption Posture Matrix:**
- High Trust, High Inevitability: The "Gold Rush." The market is ready; speed of deployment is all that matters.
- Low Trust, High Inevitability: The "Resentful Mandate." Users will adopt it because they have to, but they will look for any excuse to reject it. Requires massive "Safety" messaging.
- High Trust, Low Inevitability: The "Niche Toy." Cool tech, but no mass-market urgency.
- Low Trust, Low Inevitability: The "Rejected Paradigm." The market views it as a dangerous fad. Do not launch.

# Section 3: Quality Weighting Rules
**The 'Loss of Control' Multiplier:** Feedback that cites "Loss of Accountability," "Black Box decision making," or "Lack of Control" over a new technology is weighted at 3x. Humans will tolerate high complexity if they feel they are in control; they will reject even simple technology if it makes them feel helpless.

# Section 4: Benchmark Context
**The Crossing the Chasm Filter:** The Analytics agent must index the data against the typical "Crossing the Chasm" framework. Innovators buy technology because it is new. The Early Majority buys technology because they see Innovators proving that it works. The Analytics agent must determine exactly which "Proof Points" the Early Majority is demanding before they will cross the chasm.

# Section 5: Output Format Specification
**Section 1: Study Parameters**
Technology paradigm studied, target audience placement on the adoption curve.
**Section 2: Executive Summary**
The definitive diagnosis of the Adoption Posture (e.g., Resentful Mandate vs Gold Rush).
**Section 3: The Trust & Threat Diagnostic**
Synthesis of the deepest anxieties and job-security threats preventing organic adoption.
**Section 4: The Comprehension Gap**
How wildly the user's understanding of the technology differs from the truth.
**Section 5: Decision Map Response**
Direct mapping to the marketing pivots, 'Human in the Loop' features, or educational investments requested in the brief.
**Section 6: Limitations and Confidence Notes**
Required section.

# Section 6: Multi-Session Analysis Guide
Analyze for the "Trojan Horse." If users are terrified of "Artificial Intelligence," but love a feature called "Smart Predictive Fill," the Analytics agent must explicitly instruct the client to drop the scary paradigm nomenclature and sell the technology entirely via its boring, practical utility.

# Section 7: Flagging and Limitation Language
**When the 'Resentful Mandate' pattern is detected:**
"Synthesis of this cohort reveals a highly hostile 'Resentful Mandate' posture toward this technology. [Percentage]% of respondents view this paradigm as an inevitable, irreversible shift in their industry, but they harbor deep anxiety regarding the loss of accountability and their own job security. This cohort is passively searching for proof that the technology will fail. Launching this product with aggressive 'disruption' or 'efficiency' marketing will trigger an immediate defensive backlash. We strongly advise pivoting all messaging to emphasize 'Human Control,' 'Transparency,' and 'Safety Gates' to lower the psychological barrier to entry."
