# Evidence Reporting

## Confidence language

- Use `clear signal` when multiple sessions and evidence records support the same pattern with acceptable reliability.
- Use `directional signal` when the pattern appears but coverage is limited, contradictory, or low reliability.
- Use `insufficient evidence` when coverage or evidence quality does not justify an analytic claim.

## Findings

A good finding should answer:
- what pattern appears
- where it appears in the study
- what evidence supports it
- how confident we should be

## Recommendations

Recommendations should be:
- tied to a finding
- scoped to an actionable decision
- phrased as a next move, not a slogan

Avoid:
- generic `improve communication` recommendations
- claims that combine satisfaction with demonstrated transfer
- summaries that erase minority but important risk signals
