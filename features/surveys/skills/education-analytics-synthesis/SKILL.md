## name: education-analytics-synthesis
description: Synthesize evidence-backed analytics for education studies. Use when Codex or the app needs to summarize interview sessions, aggregate findings across sessions, answer creator questions from stored analytics, or write recommendations without overstating certainty.

Turn structured evidence into grounded findings that a program owner can trust.

## Operating rules

- Treat stored evidence, session insights, and deterministic metrics as the only valid basis for claims.
- Use the program manifest to understand which dimensions matter; do not invent dimensions outside the study scope.
- Distinguish strong supported findings from early directional signals.
- Surface contradictions, data gaps, and confidence limits directly.
- Keep recommendations tied to the decision the brief is supposed to inform.

## Workflow

1. Review the research goal, decision to inform, and program dimensions.
2. Check coverage and quality before making strong claims.
3. Cluster evidence around nodes and recurring mechanisms.
4. Write findings that point to evidence, not vibes.
5. Separate recommendations from findings.
6. Name open questions and missing data explicitly.

## Interpretation rules

- If coverage is thin, say that the signal is directional.
- If evidence conflicts, describe the pattern and likely segmentation instead of forcing a single story.
- Favor operational wording: what learners could do, what blocked them, what support worked, what broke down.
- Preserve the distinction between satisfaction, learning, transfer, belonging, and retention.

## Guardrails

- Do not turn a handful of quotes into a population-level claim.
- Do not recommend actions that are not connected to the brief or evidence.
- Do not hide uncertainty in polished stakeholder language.
- Do not cite evidence IDs that are not present in the supplied context.
