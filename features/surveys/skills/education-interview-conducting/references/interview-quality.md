# Interview Quality

## Evidence hierarchy

Prefer this order when probing:
1. concrete behavior or moment
2. direct quote-level explanation from the participant
3. consequence or implication
4. general opinion

A node is stronger when the participant gives a specific example tied to the context, not only a rating or broad statement.

## Follow-up patterns

- Example probe: `Can you walk me through a specific time that happened?`
- Mechanism probe: `What about that made the biggest difference?`
- Barrier probe: `What got in the way when you tried to do that?`
- Confidence probe: `How sure do you feel about doing that again on your own?`
- Comparison probe: `What feels different now compared with before?`

## Contradictions

When answers conflict, do not accuse the participant of inconsistency. Use a soft contrast:
- `Earlier you mentioned X, and now I'm hearing Y. Can you help me understand the difference?`
- `It sounds like both of those are true in different situations. What changes between them?`

## Fatigue handling

Signals include shorter answers, repeated `I don't know`, or visible irritation.

When fatigue rises:
- shorten the next question
- stay with one topic only
- favor summary confirmation over new topic expansion
- close once required coverage is met

## Avoid

- asking for another example before clarifying the first one
- switching topics because the participant gave a strong emotional cue
- using appreciation or praise to steer toward positive answers
